ABOUT US

Pillars of hope children’s home was founded in 2013 by Vitallis Nyambeka and a friend, concerned with the plight of homeless children. However, the friend withdrew leaving all the management to Vitallis Nyambeka and his wife Glady’s Muhonja. The primary purpose of the home is to provide a habitable environment for the growth of the vulnerable children, orphaned and those who have lost their families; providing basic needs, love, education and skills to empower them.

Learn more about us!

Introduction

In the streets of Nairobi and other towns in Kenya, you will find children roaming from one corner to another. Some are in unbearable conditions and the society has labelled them as “Chokora”. Some of these children land in the streets due to various reasons amongst them being:

  • Poverty
  • Child abuse
  • Death of parents
  • HIV and AIDS
  • Famine
  • War
  • Early pregnancy
  • Street family parenting.


Project Objectives

  • Providing love, basic needs, education, medical care and a good family environment to grow in for the children
  • reducing the number of street children in Kenya
  • Cutting down possibility of street families through integration of the children in the home
  • Ease access to education for the children in the home
  • Equipping children with the basic skills to survive in different circumstances of life
  • Continue the close working relationship with Government Rescue Centres
  • Nurturing the overall well-being of the children’s lives

Goals

  • Build a home that will raise, nurture and re-habilitate children and integrate them in the family
  • Employ trained, knowledgeable and caring staff to guide the children desirable manner both to God and society
  • Utilise the land in a way that it will become self-sustainable by building our own houses, growing our own crops, install a rainwater tank and by rearing chicken as a fund-raising project
  • Giving opportunities to willing persons to serve the children on volunteer basis within their capacities and equip them with relevant skills
  • Gain constant sources of funding to maintain the project.

Children Integration in the home

The children taken in the home include:

  • Street children
  • Orphans and vulnerable children
  • Abandoned children
  • Children who have been victims of abuse e.g. Forced marriages, victims of female genital mutilation and rape, drug abuse and alcohol abuse by parents.

The majority of children will come to our home from Government Rescue Centres, via the Kenyan Children’s Court. Children may be placed in the home by government officials after they have lost their families for one reason or another and have no one else to care for them. Some have lost their families due to ill health, death, poverty or abandonment; some have run away due to the abuse and desperation within their own homes, or some are simply lost. After all avenues of tracing for their parents or relatives to care for the children have been exhausted, POHCH will take these children into our home and care for them as if they were our own. They will attend a nearby public primary school, from class1- 8, and secondary school from Form 1-4. This home will always be there for the children, even when they leave to pursue their own dreams. They will always be able to call it their home. We will provide everything within our means that any child needs to prepare themselves to gain the most they can out of life. Counselling will be a high priority as these children have been through unbearable hardships that might have affected them especially psychologically. The Pillars of Hope children will access healthcare in the nearest public hospital as well. Basic first aid trainings will be availed for the kids to learn basic care for one another in the home as brothers and sisters and the caregivers too. We hope to acquire several first aid kits for safety in the home.
