Our Stories

Greetings, We the family of Pillars of Hope children’s home take the pleasure to introduce our ministry to you. We are a society registered under the registrar of societies to offer a home and support to vulnerable children in Kenya. Pillars of Hope children’s home has been in operation for more than five years and over the years we have been able to encounter successes and challenges in equal measures.

Strong roots unlimited growth

Invitation

We take this opportunity to invite you to find time and visit with us at the home in Athi River. We recently acquired a piece of land on agreement with one of our well-wishers and are in the process of setting up a home. It is on this ground that we wish for support and encouragement to be able to meet the needs of these children whom we are not fully able to support on our own. We kindly request that you to visit and assess our needs for yourselves and see if there is a way we can work together on how to help these needy children.

Thank you and we do look forward to your visit.

Project evauation

Once in a while we will evaluate the success of the project on the academic performance, basic interaction and response to challenges by the children. The number of children willing to stay in the home without running back to the streets could also be an indicator of the effectiveness of the program. In fund raising and farming project, we will analyse the input-output capacity of our farm and record the returns of the project. Our progress may also be indicated by the number of rooms and capacity to sustain children comfortably without undergoing strain.

Problem Statement

In a UNICEF overview, 44%of Kenyans live below poverty line. Access to basic quality services is a luxury. In another study by Kenya Children of Hope, the streets in 2007 were homes to about 250,000-300,000 children, with about 60,000 residing in the streets of Nairobi, the Capital of Kenya. The study also states that homelessness comes with other challenges, especially drug and substance abuse which affects the normal development of a child. The children do it with various reasons among them:to be able to endure pain and hunger, to release pressure from the streets, peer pressure and to sleep easily.

With all these factors in mind, establishing a home for children exposed to some of these challenges will save them from harm and give them better childhood. The children will also be empowered with education to become better citizens who can take part in nation building. Having grounds of love and family will also enable the children coexist peacefully with others hence experience a more stable social life. In Pillars of Hope the children will also access better healthcare and most important have happy childhood.
